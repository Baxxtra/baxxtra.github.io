About me

I am a researcher in Sustainable Finance, with a strong interest in how data science and financial economics intersect to address real-world challenges. My work explores how machine learning and textual analysis can inform investment decisions and enhance the sustainability practices of firms and financial institutions. I am particularly passionate about using financial data and AI as tools to foster transparency, accountability, and long-term value creation.

I am currently conducting research at the interface of sustainable investing and corporate behavior, with a focus on ESG data quality, greenwashing, and the financial implications of biodiversity and climate risks. I also enjoy contributing to teaching and supervision.

Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Technical University of Munich. Before pursuing my PhD at the University of Trento, I completed both my Master’s in Finance and Bachelor’s in General Management at EBS University in Germany. My academic path has been shaped by a desire to combine analytical rigor with a broader societal purpose—an approach I continue to follow in both research and collaboration.
